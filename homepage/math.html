Mathematics - Ancient India's Achievements

Key achievements in Mathematics

Baudhayana’s Theorem

Baudhayana, an ancient Indian mathematician, described a theorem similar to the Pythagorean theorem in his Sulba Sutras. This is one of the earliest recorded mathematical discoveries.

Example: Prove that in a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Brahmagupta’s Concept of Zero

Brahmagupta was the first to formalize operations with zero. He explained how to add, subtract, and multiply using zero, laying the foundation for modern arithmetic.

Example: Solve 354 - 0 and 0 × 25. Discuss the results.

Bhaskara’s Calculus

Bhaskara introduced concepts of calculus, such as derivatives, to explain astronomical calculations. He also developed cyclic methods for solving algebraic equations.

Example: Find the derivative of x² and explain its geometric significance.
