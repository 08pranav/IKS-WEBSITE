Astronomy - Ancient India's Achievements

Key Achievements in Astronomy

Aryabhata

Aryabhata, one of the greatest mathematicians and astronomers of ancient India, proposed the heliocentric model of the solar system. He accurately calculated the length of the year and provided a formula for the motion of celestial bodies.

Varahamihira

Varahamihira made significant contributions to astronomy and astrology. He authored the "Brihat Samhita," an encyclopedia of natural sciences, and improved techniques for predicting planetary movements and eclipses.

Brahmagupta

Brahmagupta described methods for calculating the positions of planets and the nature of solar and lunar eclipses. He also emphasized the spherical shape of the Earth.
