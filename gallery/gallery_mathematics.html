Baudhayana's Theorem

Baudhāyana listed the Pythagoras theorem in his Śulbasûtra long before Pythagoras. He demonstrated how the areas formed by the sides of a rectangle equal that formed by its diagonal, a principle central to geometry...

Brahmagupta's Concept of Zero

Brahmagupta formalized the use of zero, building on earlier work by Aryabhata. He detailed operations involving zero in his texts, revolutionizing mathematics and laying foundations for modern arithmetic...

Bhaskara's Calculus

Bhaskara introduced early concepts of calculus, exploring infinitesimals and derivatives. His works, including the Siddhānta Śiromani, showcased his profound understanding of trigonometry and planetary motion...
