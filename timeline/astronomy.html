Astronomy

1400–1200 BCE

Vedanga Jyotisha

One of the earliest Indian texts on astronomy, used to calculate lunar and solar movements for rituals and calendars.

476 CE

Aryabhata

Proposed that the Earth rotates on its axis. Accurately calculated the length of a solar year (365.358 days). Explained lunar and solar eclipses as shadows, refuting myths.

505–587 CE

Varahamihira

Authored Pancha-Siddhantika, summarizing ancient astronomical traditions. Predicted rainfall and weather patterns based on planetary positions.

598–668 CE

Brahmagupta

Introduced methods to calculate celestial positions using mathematics. Calculated Earth’s circumference and proposed the law of gravity (pre-Newton).

Medieval Period

Indian Observatories

Built by Sawai Jai Singh II (18th century), Jantar Mantar included instruments for precise astronomical observations.
