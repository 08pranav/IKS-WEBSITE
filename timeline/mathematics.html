Mathematics

800 BCE

Sulbasutras

Early texts on geometry for constructing altars. Mentioned principles akin to the Pythagorean theorem.

300 BCE

Pingala

Developed the binary number system in Chandashastra. Introduced the concept of zero as a placeholder.

476 CE

Aryabhata

Defined trigonometric functions like sine and cosine (jya and kojya). Calculated the value of π (pi) as 3.1416.

628 CE

Brahmagupta

Formalized the use of zero as a number in arithmetic. Described rules for solving quadratic equations.

1114–1185 CE

Bhaskara II

Developed early concepts of calculus in his work Siddhanta Shiromani. Proposed methods for solving indeterminate equations.
